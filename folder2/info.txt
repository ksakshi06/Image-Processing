
2D image= length * breadth
3D image=length * breadth * height
 
3D image visualization plays a crucial role in many fields by providing detailed information about the shape, 
structure, and internal features of objects. In medical imaging, it helps doctors analyze organs, detect tumors,
plan surgeries, and improve diagnosis using CT and MRI scans. In material science, 3D images are used to study
porosity, microstructures, cracks, and pore networks, enabling better material design and quality assessment. 
In engineering and manufacturing, 3D visualization supports product design, defect detection, and inspection of complex components. 
In geology and petroleum engineering, it helps analyze rock formations, pore connectivity, and fluid flow, which are essential for 
resource exploration. In robotics and computer vision, 3D data enables object recognition, navigation, and autonomous decision-making.
In architecture and construction, it aids in creating accurate models and simulations of buildings. Overall, 3D image visualization 
enhances understanding, improves decision-making, and enables more accurate analysis than traditional 2D imaging across scientific, 
industrial, and healthcare applications.
